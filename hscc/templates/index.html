What is the ACM High School Coding Competition?

The ACM High School Coding Competition is a annual programming competition held at Purdue University. Purdue's ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) Chapter hosts the competition. High School Students from around the Midwest come to Purdue to compete. Teams of two students attempt to solve 10 programming questions in the shortest possible time. The questions cover a variety of topics and difficulty levels. The best teams take home some sweet prizes.

What do I need to know?

We recommend that you are familiar with one of the following programming languages: Java, Python, C/C++, or JavaScript. Additionally having a basic knowledge of common algorithms will help tremendously. Having knowledge in the following areas can't hurt either (hint hint):

  • Strings
  • Arrays
  • Math
  • Sorting
  • Simple Graphs
  • Data Structures

What is ACM?

The Association for Computing Machinery is a national level organization for Computer Science. Because Computer Science is such a large field, ACM encompasses many sub-groups. These groups are called Special Interest Groups, SIGs for short. Each SIG specializes in a certain area of Computer Science. Here at Purdue we currently have five SIGs:

  • SIGAI: Artificial Intelligence
  • SIGAPP: Mobile App Development
  • SIGBOTS: Robotics
  • SIGGD: Game Development
  • SIGSAC: Security
