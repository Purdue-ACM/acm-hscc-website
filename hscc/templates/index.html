What is the ACM High School Coding Competition?

The ACM High School Coding Competition is an annual programming competition held at Purdue University. Purdue's ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) Chapter hosts the competition. High School Students from around the Midwest come to Purdue to compete. Teams of two students attempt to solve programming questions in the shortest possible time. The questions cover a variety of topics and difficulty levels.

When is the competition?

Saturday April 20, 2019 at Purdue University.

What do I need to know?

We recommend that you are familiar with one of the following programming languages: Java, Python, C/C++, or JavaScript. Additionally, having a basic knowledge of common algorithms will help tremendously. Having knowledge in the following areas can't hurt either (hint hint):

  • Strings
  • Arrays
  • Math
  • Sorting
  • Simple Graphs
  • Data Structures

To give an idea of the type of problems we may ask, see past year's problems here. The solutions for these problems can be found here.

What do I need to bring?

You only need to bring yourself and a teammate! We will be providing the computers and food. Additionally be sure to have a guardian with you, be it a parent or teacher.

Note: as internet access is allowed for the event, bringing materials, either electronically or in hard copy, is not allowed.

How much is it?

Thanks to our generous sponsors, this event is completely free!

Can parents attend?

Parents are welcome to attend! We will have ACM students available to answer any questions about Purdue and the CS program. However, meals will not be provided for parents, but there are plenty of places nearby to get food. Additionally, parents are not allowed to help their students during the contest.

Unfortunately we do not provide travel to Purdue. Talk to your school about providing a bus.

What is ACM?

The Association for Computing Machinery is a national level organization for Computer Science. Because Computer Science is such a large field, ACM encompasses many sub-groups. These groups are called Special Interest Groups, SIGs for short. Each SIG specializes in a certain area of Computer Science. Here at Purdue we currently have five SIGs:

  • SIGAI: Artificial Intelligence
  • SIGAPP: Applied Computing (Mobile app development)
  • SIGBOTS: Robotics
  • SIGGD: Game Development
